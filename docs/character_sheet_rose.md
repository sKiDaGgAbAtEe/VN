## Visual Non-Negotiables

Every depiction of Rose must include:
- **Glasses** — black rectangular frames (removed only in extreme emotional/vulnerable scenes)
- **Hair** — long dark-to-blonde ombré (black roots → warm caramel/blonde tips)
- **Rose Clip** — red rose hair accessory (always present except maybe in PJs/extreme grief)
- **Choker** — black collar with buckle/hardware (context-dependent; removed in PJ/off-duty mode)
- **Eyes** — striking golden/amber, catlike

### Colors
- Primary: black, charcoal, desaturated reds
- Accent: deep wine red, muted mauves, warm gold (eyes)
- Contrast: cream/ivory for skin and occasional blouse details

### Build
- Short and compact (4'10")
- Notably bustier than Alex — this is a deliberate character contrast and a narrative talking point
- Soft but sturdy; not a waif, not a pin-up

---

## Personality

### Surface
Geeky, witty, dry-humored, self-effacing

### Deep
Hyper-competent caregiver; Master's in psychology; quietly stubborn; will dismantle your denial with a pun

### Flaw
Over-responsible; minimizes her own needs; uses humor to dodge vulnerability; will burn out rather than ask for help

### Growth Arc
Learning that needing support is not a failure of competence

### Themes
- Caregiver burnout vs. pride in being the person others lean on
- The mask of humor vs. the vulnerable self underneath
- Body, beauty, and self-worth in a world that either infantilizes or fetishizes short women
- Dark humor as a love language

## Notes on Representation

Rose is a short, busty, adult Filipino woman. She must never be depicted as a child, infantilized, or fetishized. Her height and figure are part of her story — she uses them consciously, and the narrative frames this with reverence and humor, not exploitation.
